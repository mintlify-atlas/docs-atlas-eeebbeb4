The Simulated module adds several utility items that support assembly, tethering, merging, inspection, and creative-mode manipulation of physics contraptions.

## Spring

The Spring connects two blocks with a spring force. The spring pushes the connected blocks apart when compressed and pulls them together when stretched, within configurable length limits.

- Right-click a block face to set one end of the spring, then right-click a second face to complete the connection.
- Right-click with a **Wrench** to adjust the spring's strength.
- Right-click another block face **with a Spring** to increase the resting length.
- Right-click while **Shift**-clicking with a Spring to decrease the resting length.

The Spring item is also a crafting ingredient for the Torsion Spring block.

**Recipe:** Iron Sheet (top) + Iron Nugget (middle) + Iron Sheet (bottom) → 2 Springs

The Spring also exists as a **block** form — a placed spring occupies one block and connects to an adjacent block automatically. The block form drops the Spring item when broken.

<Tip>
  Press **W** on a placed Spring block to see its ponder scene (shared with the Spring item) covering length adjustment and strength configuration.
</Tip>

## Rope Coupling

The Rope Coupling is the item you use to connect a Rope Winch to a Rope Connector. Right-click a Winch or Connector with the Rope Coupling to attach one end of the rope, then right-click the paired block to complete the connection.

- Use Shears on the rope to remove it.
- Right-click while holding a Wrench to slide down a rope.

**Recipe:** String (cross pattern) + Iron Nuggets (corners) → 1 Rope Coupling

## Honey Glue

Honey Glue is a convenient alternative to Super Glue for selecting blocks to include in a Simulated Contraption.

- Right-click a block face to set one endpoint of the glued area.
- Right-click a second block face to complete the selection, creating a rectangular glued region between the two points.
- Ctrl-scroll to expand or retract the glued area along the selection axis.
- Hold **Alt** while right-clicking to place an endpoint in mid-air rather than on a block face.

Unlike Super Glue, Honey Glue does not attach to regular Create contraptions in motion. It exclusively attaches to Simulated Contraptions. When a Super Glue connection and a Honey Glue connection overlap during assembly, Honey Glue attaches to Super Glue but Super Glue does not attach to Honey Glue — use Honey Glue in complex builds to avoid unintended connections.

Honey Glue has **100 durability** and is not stackable.

<Tip>
  Press **W** on the Honey Glue item to open two ponder scenes: one for basic usage and one for its interaction with Super Glue in complex contraptions.
</Tip>

## Merging Glue

**Merging Glue** exists as both a block and an item form. Use it to permanently merge two separate Simulated Contraptions into one.

- Place the Merging Glue block on a face of one contraption facing toward a face of another contraption.
- The two contraptions merge into a single physics object.

Constraints on placement:
- Both endpoints must be on different Simulated Contraptions.
- The target face must have enough structural support.
- The two blocks cannot be at the same position and must have valid facing directions.

A Slime Ball also merges two Simulated Contraptions — right-click a contraption with a Slime Ball to merge it with the one you are holding.

## Contraption Diagram

The Contraption Diagram is an information tool that visualizes the forces acting on a Simulated Contraption.

- Right-click the Diagram on a Simulated Contraption to bind it to that contraption.
- While on the contraption, right-click to inspect it: the diagram displays the contraption's total mass, center of mass, and force arrows showing each active force and its magnitude.
- Force arrows can be set to **merged** (combined per force type) or **separate** (one arrow per force source) using the configure button.
- The Diagram must be physically located on the contraption to be inspected — you cannot inspect it while holding it off the contraption.

**Recipe:** Paper + Physics Assembler → 1 Contraption Diagram

## Plunger Launcher

The Plunger Launcher fires a rope-attached plunger. Two launched plungers from the same launcher will connect and pull their attached surfaces together, letting you manipulate Simulated Contraptions by suction.

- Right-click to fire a plunger. The plunger sticks to the first surface it hits.
- Shift right-click to remove all currently launched plungers.
- While wearing a **Backtank**, the launcher draws from air pressure instead of consuming durability.

The launcher has **200 durability**.

**Recipe:** Check JEI for the crafting recipe.

## Creative Physics Staff

The Creative Physics Staff is an epic-rarity creative-mode tool for directly manipulating Simulated Contraptions.

| Action | Effect |
|---|---|
| Left-click on contraption | Locks the contraption in place |
| Right-click on contraption | Toggles dragging the contraption |
| Hold Tab while dragging | Switches to rotation mode — move the mouse to rotate |
| Scroll while dragging | Adjusts the drag distance |

The staff is stackable to 1 and cannot be crafted. Obtain it from the creative inventory.
